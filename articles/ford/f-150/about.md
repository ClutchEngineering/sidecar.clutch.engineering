# About the Ford F-150

The Ford F-150 is America's best-selling truck and has been the top-selling vehicle in the United States for over four decades. First introduced in 1948 as part of the F-Series lineup, the F-150 has evolved into a versatile workhorse that serves everyone from construction professionals to weekend adventurers. Known for its capability, durability, and innovation, the F-150 offers a wide range of configurations including multiple cab sizes, bed lengths, and powertrains to suit virtually any need. The F-150 lineup delivers best-in-class towing, innovative features like Pro Power Onboard, and advanced technology that has redefined what a pickup truck can be.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does the Ford F-150 support Apple CarPlay and Android Auto?

Yes, the Ford F-150 supports both Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, with availability varying by model year and configuration.

**2025 F-150:**
- **Wireless** Apple CarPlay and Android Auto via SYNC 4 or SYNC 5 system
- 8-inch or 12-inch touchscreen depending on configuration
- SYNC 5 available on higher configurations with enhanced features and faster response times

**2024 F-150:**
- **Wired** Apple CarPlay and Android Auto on base configurations
- **Wireless** Apple CarPlay and Android Auto available on higher configurations with SYNC 4 system
- 12-inch touchscreen available on most configurations

**2017-2023 F-150:**
- Apple CarPlay and Android Auto introduced in 2017
- Wired connection required on most models with SYNC 3
- SYNC 4 (introduced around 2021) brought wireless connectivity to higher configurations

For older F-150 models with only wired connectivity, aftermarket wireless adapters are available that can convert the system to wireless operation by plugging into the existing USB port.

The F-150's infotainment system provides seamless smartphone integration, making it easy to access navigation, music, messages, and apps while on the road.

### What engine options are available for the Ford F-150?

The 2025 Ford F-150 continues to offer six diverse powertrain options, providing choices for efficiency, performance, and capability:

**Base Engine:**
- **3.3L Ti-VCT V6**: Naturally aspirated V6 producing 290 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque. Standard on base configurations, offering reliable performance for basic work applications.

**Turbocharged Options:**
- **2.7L EcoBoost V6**: Twin-turbocharged V6 delivering 325 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque. Provides excellent acceleration and towing capability while maintaining reasonable fuel efficiency.

- **3.5L EcoBoost V6**: Twin-turbocharged V6 producing 400 horsepower and 480 lb-ft of torque in standard form, or up to 450 horsepower in high-output applications. The most popular upgrade engine, offering outstanding towing and payload capacity.

**Traditional V8:**
- **5.0L Ti-VCT V8**: Naturally aspirated V8 generating 400 horsepower. Popular among truck enthusiasts who prefer the character and sound of a traditional V8 engine.

**Hybrid Option:**
- **3.5L PowerBoost Hybrid**: Combines a 3.5L EcoBoost V6 with an electric motor and battery pack for 430 horsepower and a class-leading 570 lb-ft of torque. Offers the best fuel economy in the lineup while enabling Pro Power Onboard capabilities.

**High-Performance:**
- **3.5L EcoBoost High-Output (Raptor)**: Specially tuned twin-turbo V6 delivering over 450 horsepower for the performance-focused Raptor model.

All engines are paired with Ford's 10-speed automatic transmission, providing smooth shifts and optimized gear ratios for efficiency and performance.

### What kind of fuel economy does the Ford F-150 get?

Fuel economy varies significantly across the F-150 lineup depending on engine choice, drivetrain configuration, and cab/bed combination:

**3.3L V6:**
- 2WD: 20 city / 25 highway / 22 combined MPG
- 4WD: 19 city / 23 highway / 21 combined MPG

**2.7L EcoBoost V6:**
- 2WD: 20 city / 26 highway / 22 combined MPG
- 4WD: 19 city / 24 highway / 21 combined MPG

**5.0L V8:**
- 2WD: 17 city / 23 highway / 19 combined MPG
- 4WD: 16 city / 21 highway / 18 combined MPG

**3.5L EcoBoost V6:**
- 2WD: 19 city / 24 highway / 21 combined MPG
- 4WD: 18 city / 23 highway / 20 combined MPG

**3.5L PowerBoost Hybrid (Best Fuel Economy):**
- 2WD: 25 city / 26 highway / 25 combined MPG
- 4WD: 24 city / 24 highway / 24 combined MPG

The PowerBoost Hybrid offers the best fuel economy in the F-150 lineup while also delivering exceptional power and torque. Real-world fuel economy can vary based on driving conditions, load, trailer weight, and driving style. The hybrid model is particularly efficient in city driving where the electric motor provides significant assistance.

### What is Ford Pro Power Onboard?

**Ford Pro Power Onboard** is an innovative integrated generator system available on the F-150 that transforms your truck into a mobile power source. This feature uses the truck's engine and battery system to generate electricity that can power tools, appliances, and equipment anywhere.

**Power Output Options:**

- **2.0 kW**: Optional on most configurations, provides enough power for basic tools and equipment
- **2.4 kW**: Available on select configurations, handles moderate power demands
- **7.2 kW** (Hybrid models only): Available exclusively on PowerBoost Hybrid F-150s, can power a job site, camping setup, or even essential home circuits during an outage

**Outlet Configurations:**

- **2.0 kW and 2.4 kW systems**: Include 400 watts of power to interior cab outlets (front instrument panel and rear center console) plus two 120-volt outlets in the truck bed
- **7.2 kW system**: Provides interior outlets plus four 120-volt outlets and one 240-volt outlet in the truck bed for high-power tools and equipment

**Key Features:**

- Functions while the truck is in motion, allowing you to charge tools and equipment while driving to the job site
- Generator mode overrides automatic idle shutdown, keeping power flowing continuously when needed
- Intelligent load management ensures safe operation and prevents overloading
- Standard on higher configurations; optional on mid-level configurations

**Common Uses:**

- Powering construction tools and equipment at job sites
- Running camping equipment, RVs, or outdoor events
- Emergency home backup power during outages (7.2 kW system)
- Operating tailgate appliances and entertainment equipment
- Charging electric vehicles or power tools

The 7.2 kW Pro Power Onboard system on PowerBoost Hybrid models is powerful enough to run essential circuits in your home during a power outage when used with a proper transfer switch, making the F-150 Hybrid an excellent emergency backup power source.

### What is the towing capacity of the Ford F-150?

The Ford F-150 offers exceptional towing capability, with maximum capacity varying based on engine, drivetrain, cab configuration, and bed length:

**Maximum Towing Capacity:**
- Up to **14,000 pounds** when properly equipped with the 3.5L PowerBoost Hybrid or 3.5L High-Output EcoBoost engine, SuperCrew cab, 4x2 drivetrain, and appropriate towing package

**Towing by Engine:**

- **3.3L V6**: Up to 8,200 pounds
- **2.7L EcoBoost V6**: Up to 10,000 pounds
- **5.0L V8**: Up to 13,000 pounds
- **3.5L EcoBoost V6**: Up to 14,000 pounds (with high-output variant)
- **3.5L PowerBoost Hybrid**: Up to 12,700 pounds (class-leading for a hybrid truck)

**Available Towing Features:**

- **Trailer Backup Assist**: Makes backing up with a trailer easier by allowing you to control the trailer's direction using a knob while the truck handles the steering
- **Pro Trailer Backup Assist**: Advanced system that can automatically steer the truck and trailer into a parking spot
- **Smart Trailer Tow Connector**: Detects trailer connection and automatically configures settings
- **Integrated Trailer Brake Controller**: Built-in brake controller for trailers with electric brakes
- **Blind Spot Information System with Trailer Coverage**: Extends blind spot monitoring to cover the length of your trailer

**Payload Capacity:**
- Maximum payload: Up to **2,440 pounds** depending on configuration

The F-150's combination of powerful engines, robust frame construction, and advanced towing technology makes it one of the most capable light-duty trucks on the market. Always consult your owner's manual and ensure your vehicle is properly equipped for your specific towing needs.
