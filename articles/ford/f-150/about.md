# About the Ford F-150

The Ford F-150 is America's best-selling truck and has been the top-selling vehicle in the United States for over four decades. First introduced in 1948 as part of the F-Series lineup, the F-150 has evolved into a versatile workhorse that serves everyone from construction professionals to weekend adventurers. Known for its capability, durability, and innovation, the F-150 offers a wide range of configurations including multiple cab sizes, bed lengths, powertrains, and trim levels to suit virtually any need. From the work-focused XL to the luxurious Limited and the off-road Raptor, the F-150 lineup delivers best-in-class towing, innovative features like Pro Power Onboard, and advanced technology that has redefined what a pickup truck can be.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What trim levels are available for the Ford F-150?

The Ford F-150 offers an extensive lineup of trim levels to accommodate different needs and budgets. For 2025, the F-150 is available in eight main trim levels:

**Work and Value Trims:**

- **XL**: The entry-level work truck starting at $38,710 MSRP, featuring essentials like Ford Co-Pilot360 safety technology, a rearview camera, and available Pro Power Onboard. Perfect for fleet and commercial applications.

- **STX**: Builds on the XL with enhanced styling, sport cloth seats, and available appearance packages for those who want a more refined look without premium pricing.

- **XLT**: The most popular F-150 trim, offering a balance of features and value with upgraded interior materials, enhanced technology options, and available convenience features like remote start.

**Premium Trims:**

- **Lariat**: Features leather-trimmed seats, dual-zone automatic climate control, an upgraded SYNC 4 infotainment system with a 12-inch touchscreen, and advanced driver-assist technologies.

- **King Ranch**: A Texas-inspired luxury trim with distinctive leather interior, unique King Ranch badging, premium materials, and comprehensive standard features.

- **Platinum**: The luxury-focused trim with quilted leather seats, advanced technology features, premium audio systems, and refined styling inside and out.

- **Limited**: The top-tier luxury model featuring the highest-end materials, technology, and comfort amenities available in the F-150 lineup.

**Specialty Trims:**

- **Tremor**: An off-road capable variant with lifted suspension, all-terrain tires, skid plates, and unique styling for adventurous drivers.

- **Raptor**: The ultimate high-performance off-road F-150 with a powerful twin-turbo V6 engine, Fox Racing shocks, aggressive styling, and track-tuned suspension for extreme terrain.

- **Lobo** (New for 2025): Features a lowered suspension, sportier bodywork, illuminated grille, and unique 22-inch wheels for a street-performance aesthetic.

### What safety features come standard on the Ford F-150?

All Ford F-150 models come standard with **Ford Co-Pilot360**, a comprehensive suite of driver-assist technologies designed to enhance safety and confidence on the road.

**Standard Ford Co-Pilot360 features include:**

- **Pre-Collision Assist with Automatic Emergency Braking**: Detects potential collisions with vehicles and pedestrians (including in low-light conditions on 2025+ models) and can automatically apply the brakes if the driver doesn't react in time.

- **Blind Spot Information System (BLIS) with Cross-Traffic Alert**: Monitors blind spots and alerts you when vehicles are detected in adjacent lanes. Cross-Traffic Alert monitors up to 33 feet behind your vehicle when backing out of parking spaces.

- **Lane-Keeping System**: Available in three modes (Alert, Aid, and Alert + Aid), this system monitors road markings using front-facing cameras and helps prevent unintentional lane departure.

- **Auto High-Beam Headlamps**: Automatically adjusts between high and low beams based on surrounding traffic and driving conditions.

- **Rearview Camera**: Provides a clear view behind the truck when reversing, with dynamic guidelines to assist with parking and trailer hookups.

**Co-Pilot360 Assist+ (available on higher trims):**

- **Intelligent Adaptive Cruise Control**: Uses radar and cameras to maintain a set following distance from vehicles ahead, automatically adjusting speed in traffic. Can also adjust cruise speed based on detected speed limit signs.

- **Lane Centering**: Works with adaptive cruise control to keep the F-150 centered in its lane on highways, reducing driver fatigue on long trips.

- **Active Park Assist 2.0**: Assists with parallel parking maneuvers using sensors and automated steering.

These advanced safety features make the F-150 one of the most technologically sophisticated trucks in its class.

### Does the Ford F-150 support Apple CarPlay and Android Auto?

Yes, the Ford F-150 supports both Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, with availability varying by model year and trim level.

**2025 F-150:**
- **All trims**: Wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto via SYNC 4 or SYNC 5 system
- **Standard trims (XL, STX, XLT)**: 8-inch or 12-inch touchscreen depending on configuration
- **Premium trims (Lariat and above)**: 12-inch touchscreen standard
- **Higher trims**: SYNC 5 with enhanced features and faster response times

**2024 F-150:**
- **XL and STX**: Typically equipped with wired Apple CarPlay and Android Auto
- **XLT and above**: Wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto available with SYNC 4 system
- 12-inch touchscreen available on most trims

**2017-2023 F-150:**
- Apple CarPlay and Android Auto introduced in 2017
- Wired connection required on most models with SYNC 3
- SYNC 4 (introduced around 2021) brought wireless connectivity to higher trims

For older F-150 models with only wired connectivity, aftermarket wireless adapters are available that can convert the system to wireless operation by plugging into the existing USB port.

The F-150's infotainment system provides seamless smartphone integration, making it easy to access navigation, music, messages, and apps while on the road.

### How reliable is the Ford F-150?

The Ford F-150 has a solid reliability record, performing well among full-size pickup trucks:

**RepairPal Rating:** The F-150 has an average annual repair cost of $788, compared to $936 for the average full-size truck and $652 for all vehicle models. F-150 owners bring their vehicles in for unscheduled repairs an average of 0.3 times per year, which matches the average for full-size trucks. The probability of a major repair issue is 15% for the F-150, compared to 18% for full-size trucks overall and 12% for all vehicles.

**Consumer Reports:** Consumer Reports expects that based on data from 2022, 2023, and 2024 models, the 2025 F-150 will have average reliability compared to other new vehicles. Newer Ford F-150s generally receive reliability scores in the 78-86% range. Ford as a brand ranked 13th out of 22 manufacturers in the 2024 Consumer Reports Reliability Survey with an "average" rating, showing a four-point improvement compared to 2023.

**Industry Recognition:** At the 2024 Kelley Blue Book Best Buy Awards, the F-150 won in its category, recognized for its combination of capability, features, and value.

The F-150's aluminum body (introduced in 2015) has proven durable while reducing weight, and Ford's continuous refinement of powertrains and technology has helped maintain the truck's reputation for dependability. With proper maintenance, many F-150s exceed 200,000 miles of service.

### What engine options are available for the Ford F-150?

The 2025 Ford F-150 continues to offer six diverse powertrain options, providing choices for efficiency, performance, and capability:

**Base Engine:**
- **3.3L Ti-VCT V6**: Naturally aspirated V6 producing 290 horsepower and 265 lb-ft of torque. Standard on XL and STX trims, offering reliable performance for basic work applications.

**Turbocharged Options:**
- **2.7L EcoBoost V6**: Twin-turbocharged V6 delivering 325 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque. Provides excellent acceleration and towing capability while maintaining reasonable fuel efficiency.

- **3.5L EcoBoost V6**: Twin-turbocharged V6 producing 400 horsepower and 480 lb-ft of torque in standard form, or up to 450 horsepower in high-output applications. The most popular upgrade engine, offering outstanding towing and payload capacity.

**Traditional V8:**
- **5.0L Ti-VCT V8**: Naturally aspirated V8 generating 400 horsepower. Popular among truck enthusiasts who prefer the character and sound of a traditional V8 engine.

**Hybrid Option:**
- **3.5L PowerBoost Hybrid**: Combines a 3.5L EcoBoost V6 with an electric motor and battery pack for 430 horsepower and a class-leading 570 lb-ft of torque. Offers the best fuel economy in the lineup while enabling Pro Power Onboard capabilities.

**High-Performance:**
- **3.5L EcoBoost High-Output (Raptor)**: Specially tuned twin-turbo V6 delivering over 450 horsepower for the performance-focused Raptor model.

All engines are paired with Ford's 10-speed automatic transmission, providing smooth shifts and optimized gear ratios for efficiency and performance.

### What is Ford Pro Power Onboard?

**Ford Pro Power Onboard** is an innovative integrated generator system available on the F-150 that transforms your truck into a mobile power source. This feature uses the truck's engine and battery system to generate electricity that can power tools, appliances, and equipment anywhere.

**Power Output Options:**

- **2.0 kW**: Optional on most trims, provides enough power for basic tools and equipment
- **2.4 kW**: Available on select configurations, handles moderate power demands
- **7.2 kW** (Hybrid models only): Available exclusively on PowerBoost Hybrid F-150s, can power a job site, camping setup, or even essential home circuits during an outage

**Outlet Configurations:**

- **2.0 kW and 2.4 kW systems**: Include 400 watts of power to interior cab outlets (front instrument panel and rear center console) plus two 120-volt outlets in the truck bed
- **7.2 kW system**: Provides interior outlets plus four 120-volt outlets and one 240-volt outlet in the truck bed for high-power tools and equipment

**Key Features:**

- Functions while the truck is in motion, allowing you to charge tools and equipment while driving to the job site
- Generator mode overrides automatic idle shutdown, keeping power flowing continuously when needed
- Intelligent load management ensures safe operation and prevents overloading
- Standard on King Ranch, Platinum, and Limited trims; optional on XLT and Lariat

**Common Uses:**

- Powering construction tools and equipment at job sites
- Running camping equipment, RVs, or outdoor events
- Emergency home backup power during outages (7.2 kW system)
- Operating tailgate appliances and entertainment equipment
- Charging electric vehicles or power tools

The 7.2 kW Pro Power Onboard system on PowerBoost Hybrid models is powerful enough to run essential circuits in your home during a power outage when used with a proper transfer switch, making the F-150 Hybrid an excellent emergency backup power source.

### What is the towing capacity of the Ford F-150?

The Ford F-150 offers exceptional towing capability, with maximum capacity varying based on engine, drivetrain, cab configuration, and bed length:

**Maximum Towing Capacity:**
- Up to **14,000 pounds** when properly equipped with the 3.5L PowerBoost Hybrid or 3.5L High-Output EcoBoost engine, SuperCrew cab, 4x2 drivetrain, and appropriate towing package

**Towing by Engine:**

- **3.3L V6**: Up to 8,200 pounds
- **2.7L EcoBoost V6**: Up to 10,000 pounds
- **5.0L V8**: Up to 13,000 pounds
- **3.5L EcoBoost V6**: Up to 14,000 pounds (with high-output variant)
- **3.5L PowerBoost Hybrid**: Up to 12,700 pounds (class-leading for a hybrid truck)

**Available Towing Features:**

- **Trailer Backup Assist**: Makes backing up with a trailer easier by allowing you to control the trailer's direction using a knob while the truck handles the steering
- **Pro Trailer Backup Assist**: Advanced system that can automatically steer the truck and trailer into a parking spot
- **Smart Trailer Tow Connector**: Detects trailer connection and automatically configures settings
- **Integrated Trailer Brake Controller**: Built-in brake controller for trailers with electric brakes
- **Blind Spot Information System with Trailer Coverage**: Extends blind spot monitoring to cover the length of your trailer

**Payload Capacity:**
- Maximum payload: Up to **2,440 pounds** depending on configuration

The F-150's combination of powerful engines, robust frame construction, and advanced towing technology makes it one of the most capable light-duty trucks on the market. Always consult your owner's manual and ensure your vehicle is properly equipped for your specific towing needs.
