# About the Land Rover Defender

The Land Rover Defender is an iconic off-road SUV that combines legendary capability with modern technology and premium comfort. First introduced in 1948 as the Land Rover Series I, the Defender name was officially adopted in 1990. After a 67-year production run, the classic Defender ended in 2016, with over two million units built. In 2020, Land Rover relaunched the Defender as an all-new model, bringing cutting-edge technology while honoring its rugged heritage and uncompromising off-road DNA.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What body styles are available for the Land Rover Defender?

The modern Defender is available in three distinct body styles to suit different needs:

- **Defender 90**: The shorter, two-door version with two rows of seating for up to five or six passengers, offering maximum maneuverability and a wheelbase of 101.9 inches.

- **Defender 110**: The four-door mid-size variant with a 119-inch wheelbase, providing the perfect balance of capability and practicality for most buyers.

- **Defender 130**: Extended over a foot longer than the 110, offering three rows of seating for up to eight passengers and significantly more cargo space.

Additionally, the **Defender OCTA** was introduced as the most powerful Defender ever made, based on the 110 body style with extreme performance capabilities.

### What engine options and trim levels does the Defender offer?

The Defender uses a "P" designation system that corresponds to horsepower and engine type:

**Engine Options:**

- **P300**: 296-horsepower 2.0L turbocharged 4-cylinder engine, providing efficient performance for daily driving and moderate off-roading.

- **P400**: 395-horsepower 3.0L turbocharged inline 6-cylinder with 48-volt Mild Hybrid Electric Vehicle (MHEV) system, delivering enhanced performance and improved fuel efficiency.

- **P500**: 493-horsepower 5.0L supercharged V8 engine, offering maximum performance for the most demanding driving scenarios.

**Trim Levels:**

The Defender lineup includes S, X-Dynamic SE, X, Outbound, and V8 trims, with pricing ranging from approximately $56,900 to over $167,800 depending on configuration. Each trim offers progressively more luxury features, technology, and capability enhancements.

### What are the Defender's off-road capabilities?

The Defender is engineered as one of the most capable off-road vehicles available, with impressive specifications:

**Key Off-Road Metrics:**

- **Wading Depth**: Up to 35.4 inches with air suspension at maximum height (33.4 inches with coil suspension), allowing confident water crossings
- **Ground Clearance**: Up to 11.5 inches at maximum air suspension height (8.9 inches with coil suspension)
- **Approach Angle**: 38 degrees (Defender 110 at off-road height)
- **Breakover Angle**: 28-29 degrees
- **Departure Angle**: 40 degrees

**Advanced Off-Road Technologies:**

- **Terrain Response 2**: Automatically detects driving conditions and optimizes vehicle settings for sand, mud, snow, rock crawling, and more
- **All-Terrain Progress Control**: Regulates vehicle speed in challenging off-road conditions, functioning like cruise control for trails
- **ClearSight Ground View**: Camera system that displays the ground beneath the vehicle on the touchscreen, helping avoid obstacles
- **Electronic Active Differential**: Distributes power to all four wheels for enhanced traction
- **Wade Sensing**: Provides real-time water depth information during deep water crossings

### How much can the Defender tow?

The Defender offers impressive towing capacity across its engine range:

- **P300 (4-cylinder)**: Up to 7,716 pounds when properly equipped
- **P400 (6-cylinder)**: Up to 8,201 pounds on the Defender 110, or 7,716 pounds on the Defender 90

**Towing Features:**

- **Advanced Tow Assist**: Provides guidance and adjusts steering assistance while reversing with a trailer
- **Hill Launch Control**: Prevents rollback when starting on steep inclines while towing
- **Gradient Release Control**: Maintains controlled descent when driving downhill with a trailer
- **Trailer Stability Assist**: Helps maintain control if trailer sway is detected

These capabilities make the Defender suitable for towing boats, campers, horse trailers, and other large recreational equipment.

### Does the Defender support Apple CarPlay and Android Auto?

Yes, the Defender supports both Apple CarPlay and Android Auto through its advanced Pivi Pro infotainment system.

**2020-2021 Models:**
- Initially launched with wired Apple CarPlay and Android Auto via USB connection

**2022+ Models:**
- Wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto became available through over-the-air software updates
- Wireless smartphone charging pad included on most trims
- Simultaneous Bluetooth pairing for two devices

The Defender's Pivi Pro infotainment system displays smartphone integration on either the 10-inch or available 11.4-inch touchscreen, with the option to also view navigation directions in the 12.3-inch digital instrument cluster.
