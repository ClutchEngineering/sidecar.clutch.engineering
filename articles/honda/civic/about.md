# About the Honda Civic

The Honda Civic is one of the most iconic and enduring compact cars in automotive history. First introduced in 1972, the Civic has evolved through eleven generations to become synonymous with reliability, fuel efficiency, and practicality. Available in sedan and hatchback body styles, the Civic offers a range of powertrains from efficient naturally aspirated and hybrid options to high-performance turbocharged variants in the Si and Type R models.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What trim levels are available for the Honda Civic?

The Honda Civic offers a diverse lineup to suit different needs and budgets:

**Standard Sedan and Hatchback Trims:**

- **LX**: The entry-level trim featuring a 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine producing 158 horsepower, LED headlights, automatic climate control, and a 7-inch touchscreen infotainment system.

- **Sport**: Equipped with a 1.5-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine delivering 180 horsepower and 177 lb-ft of torque, plus sportier styling and upgraded wheels.

- **EX**: Builds on the Sport with additional comfort features including a power moonroof, heated seats, and enhanced safety technology.

- **Touring**: The premium trim with the most advanced features and comfort amenities.

**Hybrid Models (2025+):**

Starting in 2025, Honda introduced Sport Hybrid and Sport Touring Hybrid variants featuring a combined 200 horsepower and 232 lb-ft of torque from an electrified powertrain, making them the most powerful non-performance Civics available.

**Performance Models:**

- **Si**: A sport-focused variant with a 200-horsepower turbocharged engine, sport-tuned suspension, limited-slip differential, and 6-speed manual transmission. Starting MSRP around $29,100.

- **Type R**: The ultimate performance Civic with a 315-horsepower turbocharged engine, aggressive aerodynamics, adaptive suspension, Brembo brakes, and track-focused engineering. Starting MSRP around $43,795.

[View current Civic models on Honda.com](https://automobiles.honda.com/civic)

### Does the Honda Civic come with Honda Sensing?

Yes, **Honda Sensing is standard on all Honda Civic trims**, including the base LX model. This comprehensive suite of safety and driver-assistive technologies has been standard across the Civic lineup since the 2020 model year.

**Honda Sensing includes:**

- Collision Mitigation Braking System (CMBS) with Forward Collision Warning
- Road Departure Mitigation System
- Adaptive Cruise Control with Low-Speed Follow
- Lane Keeping Assist System
- Lane Departure Warning
- Traffic Sign Recognition
- Automatic High-Beam Headlights

EX trims and above also include LaneWatch, Honda's blind spot camera system that displays the passenger-side blind spot on the infotainment screen when the turn signal is activated.

[Learn more about Honda Sensing](https://automobiles.honda.com/sensing)

### How reliable is the Honda Civic?

The Honda Civic has an excellent reliability reputation, consistently ranking among the top compact cars:

**RepairPal Rating:** The Civic earns a 4.5 out of 5.0 reliability rating from RepairPal and ranks 3rd out of 36 compact cars. The average annual repair cost is $368, significantly lower than the $526 average for compact cars. Unscheduled repairs occur an average of 0.2 times per year, compared to 0.3 times for the typical compact car.

**Consumer Reports:** Ratings vary by generation, with the current 11th generation (2022+) receiving average to above-average reliability scores. Some earlier 10th generation models (2016-2019) experienced reliability issues, particularly with transmissions and climate systems, but Honda addressed many of these concerns in subsequent years.

The Civic's reputation for longevity is well-deserved, with many examples exceeding 200,000 miles with proper maintenance.

[Read RepairPal's Civic reliability ratings](https://repairpal.com/reliability/honda/civic)

### Does the Honda Civic support Apple CarPlay and Android Auto?

Apple CarPlay and Android Auto support varies by model year:

**Wired Support (2016-2021):**
- Apple CarPlay and Android Auto were introduced in 2016
- Required USB cable connection
- Standard on most trims by 2019

**Wireless Support (2022+):**
- **Touring, Si, and Type R trims**: Wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto
- **LX, Sport, and EX trims**: Wired Apple CarPlay and Android Auto only

All 2022 and newer Civic models feature a 7-inch or 9-inch touchscreen (depending on trim) with the latest Honda infotainment system that supports smartphone integration.

### Is the Honda Civic available as a hybrid?

Yes, Honda introduced Civic Hybrid models for the 2025 model year after a long hiatus. The new Civic Hybrid is available in both sedan and hatchback body styles.

**Hybrid Specifications:**
- Combined output: 200 horsepower and 232 lb-ft of torque
- Available trims: Sport Hybrid and Sport Touring Hybrid
- Fuel economy: Up to 50 MPG city / 47 MPG highway / 49 MPG combined (sedan)

The hybrid powertrain uses a 2.0-liter Atkinson-cycle four-cylinder engine paired with two electric motors and a lithium-ion battery pack. This system provides strong acceleration while delivering exceptional fuel economy.

The Civic Hybrid represents Honda's most fuel-efficient non-electrified vehicle in the current lineup, making it an excellent choice for those seeking maximum efficiency without the range concerns of a fully electric vehicle.

### What kind of fuel economy does the Honda Civic get?

The Honda Civic offers excellent fuel economy across its entire lineup:

**Gasoline Models (2024-2025):**
- **LX (2.0L)**: 31 city / 40 highway / 35 combined MPG
- **Sport (1.5L Turbo)**: 30 city / 37 highway / 33 combined MPG
- **EX (1.5L Turbo)**: 33 city / 42 highway / 36 combined MPG
- **Touring (1.5L Turbo)**: 31 city / 38 highway / 34 combined MPG

**Hybrid Models (2025+):**
- **Sedan Hybrid**: 50 city / 47 highway / 49 combined MPG
- **Hatchback Hybrid**: 50 city / 45 highway / 48 combined MPG

**Performance Models:**
- **Si**: Approximately 27 city / 37 highway MPG
- **Type R**: Approximately 22 city / 28 highway MPG

The hybrid models offer the best fuel economy in the lineup while also providing strong performance with 200 horsepower, making them ideal for drivers who want both efficiency and engaging driving dynamics.
