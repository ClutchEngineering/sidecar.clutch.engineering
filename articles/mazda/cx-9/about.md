# About the Mazda CX-9

The Mazda CX-9 is a three-row midsize SUV that combines refined design, engaging driving dynamics, and premium features. Introduced in 2007 and fully redesigned for the 2016 model year, the CX-9 served as Mazda's flagship SUV through the 2023 model year, when it was discontinued and replaced by the CX-90.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does the Mazda CX-9 support Apple CarPlay and Android Auto?

Yes, the Mazda CX-9 supports wired Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, with factory support starting in 2019. All model years use wired connections via USB cable.

**2021-2023 Models:**
- **Wired** Apple CarPlay and Android Auto standard across all configurations
- 10.25-inch full-color touchscreen display
- Mazda Connect infotainment system with commander control dial
- Wireless smartphone charging pad standard on Touring, Grand Touring, and Signature configurations
- Four USB ports for device connectivity and charging

**2020 Models:**
- **Wired** Apple CarPlay and Android Auto
- 9-inch touchscreen on Touring, Grand Touring, and Signature configurations
- 7-inch touchscreen on Sport configuration
- USB cable connection required for smartphone integration

**2019 Models:**
- **Wired** Apple CarPlay and Android Auto standard on Touring and above
- 8-inch Mazda Connect touchscreen on higher configurations
- 7-inch touchscreen on Sport configuration
- First model year with factory Apple CarPlay and Android Auto support

**2016-2018 Models:**
- No factory Apple CarPlay or Android Auto support
- Retrofit available through Mazda dealerships for vehicles equipped with the Mazda Connect infotainment system
- Official upgrade kit available from Mazda (part number 00008FZ34)
- Aftermarket solutions also available

For models with only wired connectivity, aftermarket wireless adapters are available that plug into the existing USB port and enable wireless functionality without modification to the vehicle.
